Here is a funny fact I read about the p-value in hypothesis testing. First, we define a normal distribution with an unknown mean and a standard deviation of 1.

[latex]X\sim \mathcal{N}(\mu ,1)[/latex]

Let's say we have an observed value of 2.18 and we want to know if it supports the hypothesis of the real average being in the range (-0.5, 0.5) with a significance level of 0.05. We would calculate the p-value as follow.

[latex]X\sim \mathcal{N}(\mu ,1)[/latex]
